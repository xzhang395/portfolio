Partially Shipped, Work-In-Progress
Team of 3, my role: interaction designer


BACKGROUND

“Research shows that people frequently try to search for information with other people. The fact that no user interface for collaborative searching has yet caught fire suggests that the best parts of the design space have yet to be investigated.”

This project is sponsored by Google and NSF, with the goal to build an efficient tool for complex exploratory search task. My role as a designer on the team is to design the flow and interaction for collaboration features. (Advisor: Prof. Aniket Kittur)

This project is still work-in-progress. Want to try Beta version?

PROBLEM

People are searching collaboratively without the benefit of specialized tools

Research shows that people frequently search for information with other people in scenarios such as trip planning, shopping, apartment hunting or academic reference search.


However, there is no successful tool to support collaborative search tasks. Instead, people are making do by adapting to currently available tools for difference parts in a collaborative search process.


For this project, we want to understand what difficulties do people currently have when search collaboratively and how can we improve the experience.


RESEARCH

What can we learn from the predecessors


Research tools for collaborative search is not new. Why would people rather repurpose communication technologies than using theses specialized tools? To understand why, we did heuristic evaluation on Diigo, the only functioning collaborative search tool on the market.


While search being the primary task, using Diigo requires additional effort above and beyond other ad hoc solution. An experience map was made to highlight the pain points of using Diigo for collaborative search.

Defining the key issues

To understand current challenges related to collaborative search, we conducted research through literature review, retrospective interviews, and contextual inquiry.

Pilot study: asked groups of 2 to collaborate on apartment searching, provided them with hard copies of apartment postings and annotation tools.

Used affinity diagram to combine and sort research findings.

Combining and sorting research data with an affinity diagram led us to four key issues: awareness, Opinion and preference gathering, Note-taking, Structuring and Synthesis.


  • AWARENESS
    People are unaware of what their collaborators have done

    “ I want to know who is knowledgeable of what information and who has contributed what content.

  • OPINION AND PREFERENCE GATHERING
    Gathering opinion online is difficult, decisions are mostly made in person

    “ People rarely respond to the links I shared.

  • NOTE-TAKING
    No easy way to take or share notes

    “ To take notes, I copy and paste the useful information to a Word document. When search on mobile, I would send the content to desktop through messenger app.

  • STRUCTURING AND SYNTHESIS
    Hard to retrieve and organize most relevant information

    “ My notes are all over the place: docs, bookmarks or links in messenger. It's hard to recall where are the most useful ones.

ITERATIONS

Iterations on the four problem areas

Based on the research findings the ideation was focused on the 4 problem areas and questions:

  • Awareness

  • Opinion and preference gathering

  • Note-taking

  • Structuring and Synthesis

Awareness

Early iterations explored transparent search history, real-time screen sharing, notification and highlights from missed activities.

Major test findings:

  • Personal information should be separated from group information

  • People want to be aware of not only what others did but also why they did it

  • Notification spamming is a concern

Opinion and preference gathering

Early iterations focused on exploring how to quickly gather opinions by creating polls from saved notes.

Note-taking

We started with speed-dating storyboards to verify needs for annotation ( View storyboards and findings). People expressed needs for different annotation techniques depending on the content and the stage of the search task.

Using interactive prototype to test how people use different annotation techniques.

Structuring and Synthesis

Highly organized travel itinerary from one of our interviewee
