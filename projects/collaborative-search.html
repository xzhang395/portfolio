Collaborative Search

Collaborative Search

September 2017 - Now
Partially Shipped, Work-In-Progress
Team of 3, my role: interaction designer


How can we make collaboration in online search more efficient?

BACKGROUND

This project is sponsored by Google and NSF, with the goal to build an efficient tool for complex exploratory search task. My role as a designer on the team is to design the flow and interaction for collaboration features. (Advisor: Prof. Aniket Kittur)

This project is still work-in-progress. Want to try Beta version?

PROBLEM

People are searching collaboratively without the benefit of specialized tools

Research shows that people frequently search for information with other people in scenarios such as trip planning, shopping, apartment hunting or academic reference search.


However, there is no successful tool to support collaborative search tasks. Instead, people are making do by adapting to currently available tools for difference parts in a collaborative search process.


For this project, we want to understand what difficulties do people currently have when search collaboratively and how can we improve the experience.


RESEARCH

Is there a need for a specialized tool?

To understand current challenges related to collaborative search, we conducted research through literature review, 12 retrospective interviews, and 8 contextual inquiry.

Pilot study: asked groups of 2 to collaborate on apartment searching, provided them with hard copies of apartment postings and annotation tools.

Used affinity diagram to combine and sort research findings.


A lot duplicated searches

Without being aware of what their collaborators are searching, people end up finding and reading the same information multiple times.

Hard to retrieve important information

Most people share the information they find useful through messaging platforms. However, important information and decisions made around it tends to get lost in chat history.

Rely on in-person meeting for information exchange

Note-takin is currently unstructured, and methods varies across individuals. It is hard for one person to understand information processed by another.
Also, people are not responsive to online messages. It is hard to gather feedback.

“ My teammate's note is so unorganized that only he can understand.

“ People rarely respond to the links I shared.

DESIGN CRITERIA

How might we improve collaboration efficiency by allowing people to build on each other's findings?

A successful design should:

  • Inform user what has been done and what needs to be done.
  • Provide a structure to organize and synthesize information.
  • Help evaluate the importance of the information.

ITERATIONS

The initial iteration: chat as the central hub

The first iteration explored how chat can be the central hub for receiving update from collaborators, gather opinion and make decisions. And how can important information be filtered from chat messages.

However after testing the prototype we found that:

  • People don't want another messaging platform to check.
  • Chat is perceived to be temporary and unstructured. Filtering chat is complicated.
  • People are unwilling to switch browser that they are used to.
  • Most of the complex searches are done on desktop.

Change in direction: A chrome extension that helps structure and synthesize information

Grouping information is a natural way of synthesizing.

People like the flexibility and structure provided by moving notes across buckets

People want to be informed but not overwhelmed

User should have the freedom to access all saved notes and activities but only the most relevant information should be readily visible.

Nothing should get in the way of the reading experience

Another interesting finding is that users hate anything that will get in the way of their reading experience. We heard from users: "The pop-up tool box is distracting", "draging and droping the tags breaks my reading flow".

Using interactive prototype to test how people use different annotation techniques.

DESIGN DECISIONS

Avoid duplicated search by being aware of what's been viewed or stashed away

Continue the work of others and synthesize information as a group

Make a decision together to meet the various group members’ desires

REFLECTIONS
