Present
Context-aware Reminder

January 2018 – February 2018 (~ 1 months)
My role: Designer
Team: Dongjun (Business), Maitreyi (Developer), Parvathy (Developer)

“Present” is a beacon based smart reminder that delivers notifications according to user's context.

PROBLEM

Despite of popularity, digital reminder often fails to help users completing the tasks

Many people use scheduling or reminder apps to help them become more productive. However, they still forget things and experience inconvenience in daily life due to the lack of information.

USER RESEARCH

What makes current reminders inefficient?

Here are our top insights after interviewing 12 people ranging from age 23 to 76.

  • People ignored the notification from reminder app when busy with other tasks

    “I get notification from this fitness app but was at work so I dismissed it. Then I never remembered to do the training even after getting home

  • Current reminder only reminds user of the deadlines rather than suggesting the best time to complete the tasks

    “I always disable the auto reminders on my to-do app, it just tells me when it is due, not when I should be doing it

  • People who use sticky notes prefer to leave the notes at where the task is relevant

    “I put sticky notes on my laptop, so I’m reminded every time I open my laptop.

ITERATIONS

Paper prototypes were created to test the basic user flow and features.

Finding from initial user testing with 4 users leads to the following feedback:

  • Sensor setup is mostly a one-time process, does not need to be quickly accessible from the home screen

  • Users refer to the dashboard to check what reminder has been added or if any reminder need to be disabled before adding a new one. Scannability of the dashboard is important in this scenario

Flow

Redesign for shared reminder

One of the biggest feedback we received during testing is the concern of using the product in a shared space. People mentioned scenarios where they would like to create reminders for family members or roommates:

“Me and my roommate go to the leasing office to pay rent in turns, I always have to remind her the first day every month. It would be nice if this can automatically remind her before she leaves the apartment

“I’d like to use this to remind my grandson to clean up his room

After adding the sharing feature, we created a prototype click through for user testing. We tested it with 5 users with the Think-aloud method and asked follow-up questions afterwards.

Simple way to add new people

It wasn’t clear how this prototype allows adding new people to share the reminder with. So we want to create a most lightweight experience of assigning reminder to new collaborator.

In the new design, the user can directly add a collaborator while creating a new reminder. They can simply add the person by sending an invite to their email address.

Decline an assigned reminder

During testing some users mentioned the scenario of not able to complete the reminder assigned by others and would like to decline and leave a message explaining the reasoning.

To address this need, the user has the control to accept or decline the reminder assigned by others before the reminder is final on their timeline.


DESIGN DECISIONS

Onboarding

Login and sensor setup

A step-by-step guidance on how to get started with the app. Connect and label sensors in different locations.

Add new

Define triggers for the reminder

Create a new reminder by defining when and where it will be active. Remind family or roommates by assigning the reminder to other users.

Accept or decline

React to reminder assigned by others

Decline the assigned reminder if needed, leave a message to explain why.

View and alert

No more missed notifications

View all reminders on a timeline, swipe to quickly switch between dates. Mark as done after completing the task. Snooze the reminder to be alert another time when the triggering condition is met.
